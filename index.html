Cell Decellularization

Process

Getting beating clumps of cells to organize into a 3-dimensional heart requires scaffolding. Start with the scaffold that nature provides, using a technique called decellularization to strip away all the living cells from the heart.
Organ sits in a specialised vessel for weeks while chemicals wash away its DNA, lipids, cellular proteins, sugars and all other cellular materials. Afterwards all thatâ€™s left is a pale white matrix of collagen and other structural proteins that once held everything together.
After the donor cell is stripped, antigens are removed which can cause the body's immune system to reject the new organ. Scaffold can now be repopulated with healthy new cells that are immunologically matched to the patient.

Testing

Using a CT scanner, we try to find out if this graft can do what these structures do in real life - prevent fluids from flowing back into the heart and feed oxygenated blood from the Aorta to the artery that feed hungry heart muscles. We pump contrast fluids through the Aorta to check for backflow and to see if the artery that feed the heart muscle leak.

Vision

Process of Decellularization and Stem Cell seeding could develop better replacement parts for the heart, these parts might prove more able to repair themselves and theoretically grow a young patient. Technique could transform medicine. Helping patients and developing tools to improve lives. Using donor organs that will not ordinarily be suitable for transplantation, the goal is to regenerate organs for transplantation, organs for patients with organ failure.
